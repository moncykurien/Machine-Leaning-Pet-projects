Background Information


In a Stroop task, participants are presented with a list of words, with each word displayed in a color of ink. The participant’s task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed. The task has two conditions: a congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example RED, BLUE. In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example PURPLE, ORANGE. In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

Naming the font color of a printed word is an easier and quicker task if word meaning and font color are not incongruent. If both are printed in red, the average time to say "RED" in response to the word 'Green' in red color is greater than the time to say "RED" in response to the word 'RED' in red color.

1. Independent variable and Dependent variable


Dependant Variable: The dependant variable is the time taken by the participant to respond in seconds.

Independant Variable: The independant variable is the word condition(Congruent or Incongruent).

2. Hypotheses


Null Hypotheses: The null hypotheses is that there is no change in the response time in recognizing the congruent words vs the response time in recognizing the incongruent words. (µc = µi) or (µc - µi = 0).
µc - > mean time for the population of Congruent condition.
µi - > mean time for the population of Incongruent condition.
mean time for the population of Congruent condition and mean time for the population of Incongruent condition are same.

Alternative Hypotheses: The alternative hypotheses is that there is a significant difference in the response time in recognizing the congruent words vs the response time in recognizing the incongruent words. That is the reponse time would have either increased or decreased significantly. (µc ≠ µi) or (µc - µi ≠ 0).
µc - > mean time for the population of Congruent condition.
µi - > mean time for the population of Incongruent condition.
mean time for the population of Congruent condition and mean time for the population of Incongruent condition are different.

Kind of statistical test: I would perform a Dependant Sample t-Test on the data. This is because there is no population parameters available. We only have the sample data. Which does not fit a z-test. So I am opting the t-test for this analysis.
Since the data is collected by performing the same experiment in two different conditions, there is a chance of carryover effect. So the different conditions can be considered as dependant.
Assumptions:
-> We assume that the distribution of mean difference is normal.
-> The two samples are dependant.
-> We do not know the population mean and population standard deviation.
-> The sampling is random.
-> The differences are of measurement variable and hence paired t-test can be performed.
-> Pairs of observations are independant(individual observations are not independant).

The Cause of stroop effect


The interference between different information the brain receives is causing a problem. There are two theories that may explain the Stroop effect:

  1. Speed of Processing Theory: the interference occurs because words are read faster than colors are named.
  2. Selective Attention Theory: the interference occurs because naming colors requires more attention than reading words.
